Yes, in most jurisdictions. Spears are classified as sporting or outdoor equipment — not regulated by the ATF. No permits, background checks, or waiting periods required.

However: Check your local laws. Some states have restrictions on blade length. Verify with local law enforcement if you're unsure about your jurisdiction.

Legally? Spears fall under the same legal protections as baseball bats. You have the right to defend your home with reasonable force.

Practically? In tight spaces, the Reapr at 44 inches is maneuverable. In a hallway or open floor plan, the Assegai Long at 81 inches controls any approach. These are real weapons with real stopping power.

Any use of force in self-defense carries legal risk — regardless of the weapon. What matters:

  • You have a legal right to protect yourself and your home
  • Your actions should be proportional to the threat
  • Self-defense laws vary significantly by state

Know your local self-defense laws. Consider consulting a lawyer who specializes in use-of-force cases.
